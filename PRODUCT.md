# Product

## Register

brand

## Platform

web

## Users

Young sneaker enthusiasts aged 18–30 who are passionate about streetwear and fashion culture. They browse on mobile and laptop, typically during downtime or when hunting for a specific release. They care about how a store *looks* as much as what it stocks — a generic checkout funnel loses them, a curated visual experience keeps them scrolling.

## Product Purpose

SpreeX is an online sneaker and fashion footwear shop that doubles as a portfolio piece demonstrating frontend craft. Success means two things: a visitor adds a pair to cart and checks out, *and* a hiring manager or client looking at the project walks away impressed by the design quality and technical execution.

## Positioning

A sneaker shopping experience that feels like stepping into a concept store — not a marketplace, not a template.

## Conversion & proof

- **Primary CTA:** "Khám phá bộ sưu tập" — a soft scroll-to-browse invitation on the hero.
- **Secondary fallback:** Individual product cards with "Xem chi tiết" leading to the detail page, lowering commitment.
- **The line a visitor remembers after 10 seconds:** "This store looks premium — these are real sneakers, not AliExpress."
- **Belief ladder:** (1) The store looks legit and high-end → (2) The products are authentic and curated → (3) The checkout is safe and the delivery is fast → (4) I trust this enough to enter my payment details.
- **Proof on hand:** 30-day return policy badge, free shipping promo, demo payment integrations (VNPay, MoMo). No testimonials or press yet — the 3D product showcase and polished UI *are* the proof at this stage.

## Brand Personality

**Energetic, youthful, bold.** The voice is sporty-casual — confident without being aggressive, playful without being childish. It speaks like a friend who knows sneakers, not a luxury house that talks down to you.

## Anti-references

- **Lazada / Shopee / Tiki:** Cluttered, price-first, promotional banners everywhere, cheap visual treatment. SpreeX is the opposite of a marketplace bargain bin.
- **Overly minimal / empty:** Stark white pages with tiny text and no imagery. The store should feel alive, not like an art gallery with nothing on the walls.

## Design Principles

1. **Concept store, not catalog.** Every screen should feel curated and intentional, not auto-generated from a product database.
2. **Motion earns attention.** The 3D hero model, scroll transitions, and hover states exist because they pull visitors deeper — not because "animations look cool."
3. **Trust through craft.** Premium visual quality signals product authenticity. A polished interface is itself proof that the store is legitimate.
4. **Show the product, not the chrome.** UI elements recede; sneakers and imagery lead. Typography and color support the product photography, never compete with it.
5. **Portfolio-grade execution.** Every technical choice — the Spline 3D integration, the glassmorphic panels, the responsive grid — should demonstrate frontend skill without being gratuitous.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA compliance for text contrast. Reduced-motion alternatives for all animations. Keyboard-navigable interactive elements.
